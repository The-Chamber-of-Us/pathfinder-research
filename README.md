# Pathfinder Research

> Structured research spikes that turn field observations, implementation questions, and strategic uncertainties into evidence-traceable working models for TCUS decisions.

## Purpose

Pathfinder Research is a lightweight research workflow for **The Chamber of Us**.

It starts with real observations from partners, field collaborators, technical advisors, model outputs, project work, or unresolved implementation questions, then turns those observations into focused research spikes.

The goal is not polished certainty.

The goal is to reduce uncertainty responsibly, preserve the evidence trail, and develop working models that can guide TCUS decisions without pretending that early research is final truth.

## What This Repository Is

This repository is a research bench.

It is used to:

- test claims before they become strategy
- compare independent model generations
- separate evidence from interpretation
- identify what needs local or expert verification
- preserve uncertainty instead of hiding it
- convert exploratory research into decision-ready working findings
- build reusable adoption-pathway methods for TCUS work

The repository is especially focused on construction innovation, adoption pathways, institutional trust, and implementation readiness in Ethiopia, with current emphasis on floor systems, low-carbon building materials, and Nebyu Samuel Haile’s vaulted earthen floor system.

## What This Repository Is Not

This repository is not a collection of final reports.

It is not a place where AI-generated outputs are treated as truth.

It is not optimized for broad literature reviews, polished advocacy, or success stories.

Raw model outputs are treated as leads. They only become useful after cross-model review, evidence cleanup, human review, and local/expert verification.

## Emerging Working Model

The repo is currently helping TCUS test an emerging adoption hypothesis:

> Construction innovations may diffuse when actor-specific evidence reduces uncertainty for successive independent decision-makers. Adoption failure may occur even when evidence exists if that evidence does not reduce uncertainty for the actor controlling the next transition.

A related working concept is **Second-Decision Survival**:

> A construction pilot becomes an adoption pathway only when the evidence it produces enables another actor, not merely the original inventor or sponsor, to make a new commitment.

Examples of second independent decisions include:

- a local engineer reviews or signs off
- a second host agrees to test or use the system
- a contractor agrees to build it again
- a donor funds a follow-on project
- a public agency procures it again
- a standards body begins formal review
- a university incorporates it into teaching, testing, or curriculum
- a supplier produces the material or component again
- a project is redesigned based on lessons and attempted again

This is a hypothesis to test, not a doctrine to promote.

## Working Principles

- Start with a real-world trigger.
- Separate evidence from interpretation.
- Preserve raw AI outputs as leads, not truth.
- Compare multiple model generations before extracting findings.
- Convert strong claims into evidence-backed working findings.
- Track claims that need verification.
- Use human and local expert review before making confident claims.
- Prefer useful uncertainty reduction over broad, polished memos.
- Treat disagreement between models as signal, not noise.
- Preserve rival explanations.
- Do not mistake first demonstration for adoption.

## Workflow

A typical Pathfinder spike moves through these stages:

1. **Trigger**
   - A question, field observation, partner comment, technical uncertainty, or implementation concern emerges.

2. **Prompt**
   - The question is converted into a structured research prompt.

3. **Independent model generations**
   - Multiple models generate independent research outputs.

4. **Cross-model comparison**
   - The outputs are compared for convergence, contradiction, source quality, and missing evidence.

5. **Cleaned findings**
   - The strongest working findings are extracted, with uncertainty preserved.

6. **Claims needing verification**
   - Important unresolved claims are turned into a verification queue.

7. **Human review**
   - TCUS or local reviewers add judgment, context, concerns, and next questions.

8. **Final or working synthesis**
   - When enough evidence exists, the spike may produce a reviewed working output.

## Evidence Rule

The source of truth is not the AI output.

The source of truth is the evidence trail.

Strong claims should eventually be supported by:

- source titles and authors
- URLs
- page numbers where available
- exact quotes where useful
- evidence tiers
- epistemic status
- confidence level
- local verification notes
- unresolved contradictions

Use these epistemic labels where helpful:

- documented fact
- reported claim
- observation
- technical inference
- institutional inference
- interpretation
- unresolved question
- contradictory evidence
- no public evidence found in this pass

## Evidence Tiers

### Tier A

- government documents
- university theses
- technical reports
- standards
- regulatory documents
- engineering evaluations
- procurement documents
- formal post-occupancy or monitoring reports

### Tier B

- institutional publications
- company reports
- conference papers
- donor reports
- reputable media
- NGO reports

### Tier C

- blogs
- social media
- promotional material
- project photo captions
- unverified claims

Tier C sources can be useful leads, but they should not carry major claims without stronger support.

## Review Standard

A claim should not be promoted to a cleaned finding just because a model stated it confidently.

Before accepting a claim, ask:

- Which source supports it?
- Is the source primary, secondary, or promotional?
- Does another model contradict it?
- Does the claim depend on a fuzzy term?
- Does it require local verification?
- What decision would this claim affect?
- What happens if the claim is wrong?

## Why This Matters

TCUS is trying to understand how promising ideas become real, trusted, repeatable systems.

For construction innovation, the hard question is rarely only:

> Can the technology work?

The harder question is often:

> Can the technology become trusted, buildable, approvable, procurable, maintainable, and repeatable inside a real delivery system?

Pathfinder Research exists to study that gap.
