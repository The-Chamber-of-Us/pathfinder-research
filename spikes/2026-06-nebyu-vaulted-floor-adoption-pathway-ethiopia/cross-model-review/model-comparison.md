## Purpose

Compare the ChatGPT Deep Research and Gemini Pro Extended generations for the Nebyu vaulted floor adoption-pathway spike.

This is not the cleaned findings file yet. It is a comparison layer that identifies where the two model outputs agree, where they differ, which claims look strongest, which claims are too confident, and what should carry forward into the next spike artifacts.

The purpose of this spike is not to ask whether Nebyu’s vaulted earthen floor system should be adopted. The purpose is to understand the most plausible pathway by which a system like this could progress from research and testing toward a real, institutionally supported building in Ethiopia, if it progresses at all.

## High-Level Read

The two model generations agree on the core pathway logic:

> Nebyu’s system is not ready for broad adoption, but it may be ready for a carefully staged transition from research evidence into an institutionally hosted, evidence-generating first building.

Both models reject a direct path from research to market. They converge on an intermediate pathway involving:

- a trusted host institution
- a local technical reviewer or engineer
- a first building that generates evidence, not just visibility
- contractor learning and construction documentation
- a coalition that carries risk and credibility
- a second project pathway, not a one-off demonstration

The strongest shared conclusion is:

> The most plausible next pathway is not commercialization, ministry adoption, or immediate NGO deployment. It is a university-linked or research-hosted demonstration pathway that creates technical, institutional, and implementation evidence for a second project.

## Strongest Areas of Agreement

### 1. The system is technically ahead of its institutional pathway

Both models find that the technical story is more mature than the institutional story.

Current supported maturity includes:

- MIT thesis / academic research
- IASS technical paper
- 3 m prototype testing
- analytical models
- cost/carbon comparisons
- reported 15 m² full-scale Addis Ababa slab test in the ChatGPT generation

Current missing or weakly evidenced maturity includes:

- real demonstration building in use
- Ethiopian engineer-of-record precedent
- municipal approval precedent
- contractor-built repeatable method
- QA / inspection checklist
- construction manual
- post-occupancy data
- repeat project evidence

**Comparison read:** Strong. This should become a headline cleaned finding.

### 2. The next gate is trust transfer, not adoption

Both models emphasize that the next meaningful step is not adoption.

The next gate is a trust-transfer step in which technical credibility moves from the researcher/MIT context into an Ethiopian institutional context.

Trust transfer likely requires:

- Ethiopian academic host or comparable local institution
- local structural reviewer or review panel
- a real but bounded building
- monitoring and documentation
- construction process evidence
- a pathway to a second build

**Comparison read:** Strong. This is probably the central finding of the spike.

### 3. The university-led pathway is the most plausible first pathway

Both models identify a university-linked pathway as the strongest near-term route.

Why:

- existing MIT-EiABC relationship appears to be part of the public record
- universities can host research-stage demonstrations more naturally than NGOs or private clients
- university-hosted builds can create evidence without immediately demanding routine market adoption
- academic institutions can provide credibility, student learning, technical review, and documentation

**Comparison read:** Strong. The most plausible pathway is not “NGO adopts,” but “university-linked demonstration creates evidence that makes NGO/donor/government conversations more realistic.”

### 4. The first meaningful building should be functional, bounded, and evidence-generating

Both models reject two extremes:

- a symbolic pavilion that produces visibility but little adoption momentum
- an occupied home, classroom, clinic, or multi-story building that creates too much institutional and life-safety burden too early

They converge on:

- auxiliary structure
- workshop
- storage / machinery shed
- university or TVET building
- controlled rural/community structure after a first host is secured
- building with actual use and monitoring, not purely a visual demo

**Comparison read:** Strong. This should carry into cleaned findings.

### 5. The first build must create a second project

Both models recognize the dead-end demo problem.

A first building is useful only if it produces evidence that enables the next decision.

Evidence for the next decision may include:

- construction manual
- time/cost audit
- contractor lessons
- QA checklist
- monitoring results
- local engineer review
- user/maintenance data
- post-rainy-season durability observations
- improved BOQ

**Comparison read:** Strong. The first building should be judged by whether it creates credible second-project conditions.

### 6. A coalition is required

Both models agree that Nebyu alone cannot carry the system into institutional practice.

Minimum coalition logic:

- Nebyu / technical originator
- MIT or research credibility holder
- EiABC / Ethiopian institutional host
- local structural engineer or review panel
- contractor / mason team
- modest funder or donor
- possibly ministry or standards observer later

**Comparison read:** Strong. This should become a key cleaned finding.

### 7. Evidence that creates replication is different from evidence that reduces technical uncertainty

Both models distinguish different evidence functions.

Technical evidence reduces uncertainty for engineers.

But adoption momentum requires other evidence:

- construction sequence
- contractor training
- cost/time audit
- repeatable tolerances
- maintenance plan
- visible functioning structure
- institutionally trusted host
- local review

**Comparison read:** Strong. This is an important conceptual contribution.

## Main Tensions Between the Models

### Tension 1: How much weight to put on the 15 m² Addis test

ChatGPT treats the MIT-reported 15 m² full-scale slab test as a major status update.

Gemini’s generation emphasizes the 3 m prototype and does not give the same weight to the 15 m² test.

**Resolution:**  
The cleaned findings should say:

> Public evidence indicates the system has advanced beyond thesis and 3 m prototype work, including a reported 15 m² full-scale slab test in Addis Ababa. However, the lack of a detailed public test dossier means this does not yet close partner-grade review questions.

### Tension 2: Is the best first building a test bay or auxiliary structure?

ChatGPT favors a university-hosted auxiliary/workshop-scale building with a real occupiable vaulted floor under controlled monitoring, while still acknowledging test bays.

Gemini favors a single-story auxiliary structure as the most plausible first building, but sometimes argues a pure test pavilion has low adoption value.

**Resolution:**  
The cleaned findings should separate:

- **technical next step**: if the 15 m² test dossier is inadequate, do a test bay / structural mock-up
- **adoption-pathway next step**: once the technical packet is strong enough, do a university-hosted auxiliary/workshop structure

### Tension 3: Who is the first trust-carrying champion?

ChatGPT says the most likely trust-carrying champion is an Ethiopian university-linked academic/engineer.

Gemini says donors/NGOs may be strong champions, while engineers are more likely gatekeepers.

**Resolution:**  
For the first institutionally credible build, the likely champion is a university-linked Ethiopian technical actor. Donors can fund and amplify, but they do not substitute for local technical trust.

### Tension 4: Rural pathway vs university pathway

Gemini sees an NGO/donor rural pathway as medium-high probability after a university step or as a possible demonstration route.

ChatGPT sees the university-led pathway as clearly strongest, with NGO/donor as second-wave partners.

**Resolution:**  
The most plausible sequence is:

1. university-linked host / review / auxiliary build
2. NGO or donor-supported second project
3. only later, broader replication

Rural community buildings may be strong second projects, but they are probably not the cleanest first institutional gate unless the university/EiABC path is unavailable.

### Tension 5: How strongly to claim regulatory barriers

Gemini often states that municipal permitting would reject the system or that universities can bypass permitting.

ChatGPT is more cautious and treats permit pathway as a local-review question.

**Resolution:**  
Use Gemini’s regulatory concern as a hypothesis. Do not claim that permitting will reject or that university hosts bypass regulation without local confirmation.

## Proposed Adoption-Pathway Logic

The most plausible pathway today is:

1. **Evidence consolidation**
   - assemble thesis, IASS, 15 m² test summary, drawings, test data, and known limitations

2. **Local technical review**
   - identify Ethiopian university-linked structural reviewer or panel

3. **University-hosted auxiliary build**
   - build something functional, bounded, and monitored

4. **Construction learning package**
   - produce method statement, construction manual, QA checklist, time/cost audit, lessons learned

5. **Second project with NGO/donor or community-serving use**
   - classroom/training/community/workshop building only after the first build generates credible evidence

6. **Policy/standards observation**
   - invite ministry or standards-body observers after evidence exists

7. **Repeatable delivery pathway**
   - only later move toward contractor training, design guide, approval precedent, and broader adoption
