## Purpose

This file distills the strongest findings from the ChatGPT Deep Research and Gemini Pro Extended generations for the Nebyu vaulted floor adoption-pathway spike.

It is not a final reviewed pathway brief.

It is not a technology promotion memo.

It is a working synthesis for identifying the most plausible progression mechanisms, trust pathways, host environments, coalition roles, and evidence-generating opportunities available to Nebyu’s system today.

## Executive Finding

The strongest cleaned finding is that Nebyu’s vaulted earthen floor system appears technically ahead of its institutional and implementation pathway.

The public evidence supports a serious research trajectory: MIT thesis, IASS paper, physical prototype work, and a reported 15 m² full-scale slab test in Addis Ababa. But the available model outputs do not show a public, institutionally supported demonstration building, permit precedent, engineer-of-record acceptance, contractor delivery package, construction manual, QA checklist, or post-occupancy monitoring record.

That means the most plausible pathway is not:

> Research → Product → Market adoption

It is closer to:

> Research evidence → Ethiopian technical review → university-hosted monitored building → construction learning package → second project with NGO/donor/community use → later standards or policy engagement

The next gate is **trust transfer**, not adoption.

## Cleaned Finding 1: The current maturity is uneven

The system has meaningful technical maturity, but lower institutional and implementation maturity.

### Technical maturity

Relatively strong.

Supported by:

- MIT thesis / academic research
- IASS paper
- analytical modeling
- 3 m prototype work
- reported 15 m² full-scale Addis Ababa slab test

### Institutional maturity

Still early.

Missing or not publicly established in the model outputs:

- local Ethiopian engineer-of-record acceptance
- permit precedent
- institutional host agreement for a real building
- standards-body recognition
- ministry pathway
- NGO or donor commitment for a first real building

### Implementation maturity

Still early.

Missing or not publicly established:

- contractor-built repeatable process
- construction manual
- method statement
- labor/time audit
- QA and inspection checklist
- local BOQ
- training pathway
- maintenance and monitoring record

### Working implication

The adoption pathway should focus on converting technical credibility into institutional and implementation credibility.

## Cleaned Finding 2: The next gate is trust transfer

Both models converge on the idea that the system needs a trust carrier.

The current trust base is mostly academic:

- Nebyu
- MIT
- thesis / papers
- prototype evidence
- reported full-scale test

But for a real Ethiopian building, trust must move into an Ethiopian institutional context:

- EiABC / AAU or comparable university host
- local structural engineer or review panel
- contractor or mason team
- modest funder or sponsor
- later, government or standards observer

### Working implication

The question is not only “does the system work?”

The next question is:

> Who in Ethiopia can credibly stand next to it?

## Cleaned Finding 3: The university-led pathway is the strongest near-term pathway

Both model outputs identify a university-linked pathway as the most plausible first institutional route.

This pathway is strongest because:

- existing MIT/EiABC connection appears to be part of the public record
- universities can host monitored research-stage demonstrations more naturally than NGOs or commercial developers
- a university setting can support technical review, student learning, monitoring, documentation, and credibility transfer
- a university-hosted project can create evidence before asking NGOs, ministries, or contractors to carry larger risk

### Proposed university-led sequence

1. Consolidate technical evidence.
2. Secure Ethiopian academic host or review partner.
3. Identify a local structural reviewer or review panel.
4. Build a monitored auxiliary/workshop-scale structure.
5. Publish a construction and monitoring package.
6. Use that standing evidence to approach NGO/donor/community second-project partners.

### Working implication

The most plausible first host is not likely to be a housing NGO or ministry. It is more likely to be EiABC, AAU, a TVET college, or a comparable research/education institution.

## Cleaned Finding 4: NGO and donor pathways are plausible second-wave pathways

NGOs and donors may be important, but they are less plausible as the first institution to carry structural trust.

They may be drawn to:

- affordable housing relevance
- local materials
- cement and steel reduction
- carbon reduction
- workforce development
- climate and development framing

But they may hesitate because:

- beneficiary-facing buildings create reputational and safety risk
- structural floor systems are harder to justify than wall or material substitutions
- NGO program teams may not have internal structural review capacity
- donor funding does not substitute for local engineering acceptance

### Working implication

NGOs and donors are better approached after the technical and university-host pathway has produced a bounded evidence package.

A donor can fund the evidence-generating step, but should not be asked to substitute for technical trust.

## Cleaned Finding 5: Engineer-led and standards-led pathways are important but unlikely first moves

A local engineer or engineering firm will eventually be essential.

However, both models suggest that a commercial engineer-led path is less likely as the first move because it requires someone to carry professional liability for an unstandardized system before enough local evidence exists.

Similarly, standards bodies and ministries matter later, but usually do not lead first-of-kind construction-system adoption.

### Working implication

The near-term goal should be:

> Get a respected local technical reviewer involved early.

Not:

> Ask a standards body or ministry to recognize the system now.

## Cleaned Finding 6: The first meaningful building should be functional, bounded, and adoption-useful

Both outputs reject a purely symbolic first build.

A first building should be judged by whether it creates the next project.

The best first building is likely not:

- a decorative pavilion
- an occupied house
- a full classroom
- a clinic
- a multi-story building
- a commercial product showcase

The strongest first-building candidate is:

> a university-hosted auxiliary, workshop, storage, training, or similar bounded-use structure that includes a real vaulted floor condition and is monitored.

This kind of building can generate:

- technical learning
- institutional learning
- contractor learning
- QA lessons
- cost/time data
- maintenance observations
- visibility among students, faculty, and visitors
- a credible basis for a second project

## Cleaned Finding 7: A test bay may still be needed, depending on the 15 m² test dossier

The two models diverge slightly on whether the immediate next physical step should be another test bay or a functional auxiliary building.

The cleaned synthesis is:

- if the 15 m² Addis Ababa test has a detailed technical dossier that is reviewable, the next adoption-useful step may be a monitored university-hosted auxiliary/workshop structure
- if the 15 m² test dossier is unavailable, incomplete, or not strong enough for local review, then an instrumented test bay or structural mock-up may still be needed before a functional first building

### Working implication

Do not decide the first building type until Nebyu’s existing full-scale test evidence is understood.

## Cleaned Finding 8: Pavilions may create excitement but little adoption progress

A pavilion can be useful if it is instrumented and designed to answer specific structural or construction questions.

But a pavilion that is primarily visual can become a dead-end demo.

It may fail to prove:

- floor loading
- serviceability
- waterproofing
- enclosure
- MEP integration
- contractor repeatability
- inspection logic
- path to a second project

### Working implication

The first build should not be selected for visual appeal alone. It should be selected for adoption value.

## Cleaned Finding 9: The first build must generate a construction learning package

Both models agree that the adoption pathway depends heavily on repeatable execution.

The most important next evidence may not be another paper.

It may be:

- visual construction manual
- time-lapse documentation
- method statement
- tolerance guide
- formwork/decentering sequence
- QA checklist
- repair/rejection rules
- cost/time audit
- local BOQ
- contractor lessons learned

### Working implication

The first build should be designed as a documentation event.

A successful structure without a construction learning package may not create replication.

## Cleaned Finding 10: The best first champion is likely a local university-linked technical actor

ChatGPT emphasizes a local university-linked academic/engineer as the strongest first trust-carrying champion.

Gemini gives more weight to donors/NGOs as champions.

The cleaned synthesis is:

- donors can fund and amplify
- NGOs can host or replicate later
- MIT can provide research credibility
- Nebyu provides technical authorship
- but a local Ethiopian university-linked technical actor is probably the key first trust carrier

### Working implication

Find the Ethiopian technical person or institution willing to review, host, or stand next to the work.

That person or institution is more important than a polished adoption narrative.

## Cleaned Finding 11: The minimum viable coalition is more than Nebyu plus a funder

A first institutionally credible project likely requires at least:

1. **Technical originator**
   - Nebyu
   - MIT / research advisor context

2. **Ethiopian institutional host**
   - EiABC, AAU, TVET college, or comparable research/education host

3. **Local technical reviewer**
   - structural engineer, faculty reviewer, review panel

4. **Implementer**
   - contractor, mason crew, supervised construction team

5. **Modest funder**
   - donor, university grant, embassy fund, climate/housing innovation fund

6. **Optional observer**
   - ministry, standards body, municipal professional, donor technical advisor

### Working implication

A donor alone cannot solve the pathway. A technical coalition must precede or accompany funding.

## Cleaned Finding 12: Ribbed/HCB is useful because it is a floor-system precedent, but it does not transfer cleanly

Earlier Ethiopian ribbed/HCB adoption matters because it shows that floor-system innovations become ordinary through:

- manuals
- drawings
- training
- public-program use
- engineering familiarity
- repeated projects

But Nebyu’s system differs because:

- it changes structural behavior more radically
- it relies on compression geometry and masonry accuracy
- it may require different review and inspection logic
- it may not fit existing engineering routines as easily as ribbed/HCB

### Working implication

Use ribbed/HCB as a pathway precedent, not as proof that Nebyu’s system will be accepted.

## Cleaned Finding 13: SUDU is a useful but dangerous analogue

Gemini strongly uses SUDU as an analogue for university-hosted earthen/vaulted construction in Ethiopia.

The useful lesson is:

> An academic host can make an ambitious earthen building visible and credible.

The danger is:

> A successful academic demonstration can still fail to become a repeatable construction system.

Before using SUDU as a major precedent, verify:

- what exactly was built
- who hosted it
- what role EiABC played
- whether it involved floor/roof systems comparable to Nebyu
- what made it difficult to repeat
- whether artisan complexity or soil sourcing actually stalled replication

### Working implication

SUDU should be treated as a hypothesis-generating analogue, not settled evidence.

## Cleaned Finding 14: Nubian Vault, Auroville, and ETH HiLo offer institutional lessons, not direct proof

These analogues are useful because they show adoption mechanisms:

- training
- manuals
- certification
- institutional host
- living lab
- monitored use
- visible demonstration
- staged dissemination

But they do not prove Nebyu’s system is ready.

Each analogue has limits:

- Nubian Vault is mostly roof/shelter logic in Sahelian/dry-climate contexts, not Ethiopian urban floor systems
- Auroville is a long-running institutional training ecosystem, not a quick pilot
- ETH HiLo is a high-resource research/living-lab environment, not a low-cost Ethiopian delivery context
- Dieste relied on a rare engineer-contractor model that may not be replicable

### Working implication

Use analogues to identify mechanisms, not to imply equivalence.

## Cleaned Finding 15: The pathway could stall even after a successful first building

The models agree that the first building is not enough.

The pathway can stall if:

- no local engineer takes the next review step
- the building remains an academic showcase
- construction requires too much expert supervision
- contractor cost/time data is unfavorable
- the first building is too symbolic
- there is no second project target
- no manual or training package emerges
- the first host does not transfer trust to another institution

### Working implication

The first build should be planned backward from the second project.

## Cleaned Finding 16: The most plausible pathway today

The most plausible current pathway is:

## University-Led Evidence Pathway

**Stage 1: Evidence consolidation**

Nebyu assembles:

- thesis
- IASS paper
- 15 m² test summary
- drawings
- material data
- load protocol
- known limitations
- construction photos
- available monitoring data

**Stage 2: Ethiopian technical review**

A local university-linked structural reviewer or panel reviews the evidence and defines what would make a first building acceptable.

**Stage 3: University-hosted monitored auxiliary build**

A bounded-use, monitored structure is built on or through a trusted educational institution.

**Stage 4: Construction learning package**

The build produces:

- method statement
- construction manual
- QA checklist
- cost/time audit
- lessons learned
- maintenance protocol
- monitoring results

**Stage 5: Second project with NGO/donor/community partner**

A more public, service-oriented building is considered only after the first build produces transferable evidence.

**Stage 6: Later standards / ministry / broader adoption**

Only after repeated evidence does the system become a candidate for wider institutional or policy recognition.

## Current Recommended Next Move

Nebyu should not try to optimize the adoption story first.

He should identify:

1. What exact evidence already exists from the 15 m² test.
2. Who in Ethiopia can review it.
3. Which host institution could support a bounded first building.
4. Which first building would create a credible second project.
5. What construction documentation must be produced during the build.

## What Should Carry Into the Final Reviewed Adoption Pathway

## Bottom Line

The strongest cleaned synthesis is:

> Nebyu’s most plausible pathway is a university-led trust-transfer pathway: consolidate the technical evidence, secure local Ethiopian technical review, build a monitored auxiliary/workshop-scale structure through a trusted academic host, document construction and performance, and use that evidence to unlock a second project with NGO, donor, or community partners.

This pathway does not assume adoption.

It identifies the next credible mechanism by which adoption could become more plausible.
