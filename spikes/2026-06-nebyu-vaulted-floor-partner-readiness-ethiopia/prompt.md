# Deep Research Task: Partner Readiness & First-Pilot Decision Reality for Nebyu’s Vaulted Floor System in Ethiopia

## Purpose

This is an evidence-first partner-readiness spike.

* It is not a pitch.
* It is not a technology promotion memo.
* It is not asking whether the system should be adopted.

The task is to determine what Nebyu Samuel Haile should understand and prepare before approaching potential partners, such as Habitat for Humanity, universities, NGOs, donors, engineers, contractors, or government-linked actors, about a first monitored building using his vaulted earthen floor system in Ethiopia.

**The central question is:**

> What must be known, proven, clarified, or framed before a serious institution would even consider engagement with a first monitored pilot building?

* Do not assume adoption is possible.
* Do not assume a “pilot” is automatically attractive.
* Do not assume technical merit is enough.
* Do not collapse uncertainty into a clean success pathway.

**If evidence is missing, say so.**

---

## Working Premise

Nebyu’s vaulted floor system is not just a material substitution.

It is a **construction-system innovation** because it proposes to replace or reduce reliance on a reinforced concrete floor slab through:

* geometry
* compression behavior
* earthen masonry
* accurate construction
* controlled detailing
* structural confidence
* repeatable contractor execution

That makes it different from wall blocks, panels, cladding, or low-carbon cement.

A floor-system innovation faces a higher trust threshold because failure could affect structural safety, liability, permitting, occupancy, and professional responsibility.

## Priority Ordering Rule

Do not treat all questions as equally important. At the end, classify findings into three groups:

1.  **Must Know Before Serious Partner Outreach:** These are blockers. Without them, outreach risks being premature or confusing.
2.  **Useful But Not Required Yet:** These would improve the conversation but should not block first discovery meetings.
3.  **Downstream / Later Gate:** These matter for code recognition, commercialization, or scale, but not for the first partner-readiness step.
