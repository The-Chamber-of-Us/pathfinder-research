# Permission Chain and Adoption Pathway of Ribbed / HCB Slab Systems in Ethiopia

## Status

Model-generation phase in progress.

ChatGPT 5.5 Thinking Deep Research and Gemini Pro Extended Deep Research generations are complete.

No Claude generation is planned for this spike at this time.

## Relationship to Previous Spike

The previous spike asked whether ribbed / HCB slabs appear to have become normalized in Ethiopian condominium or building practice.

This spike asks a different question:

> What evidence exists for each stage of normalization, who was involved, and how did a structural floor system become acceptable and ordinary in Ethiopian construction practice?

## Purpose

The purpose of this spike is to reconstruct, as carefully as evidence allows, the permission chain and adoption pathway for ribbed / hollow concrete block (HCB) slab systems in Ethiopia.

The goal is not to prove that ribbed / HCB slabs were adopted through a clean, linear pathway.

The goal is to identify what can actually be supported by evidence, what remains unknown, and what lessons may or may not transfer to Nebyu Haile’s vaulted floor system.

## Current Working Caution

The strongest emerging distinction is between:

1. generic ribbed / hollow slab code permissibility, which appears to predate IHDP; and
2. the specific Ethiopian low-cost housing implementation package involving precast beams, HCB infill, manuals, procurement, MSE training, and repeated condominium use.

Do not collapse these into one claim.
