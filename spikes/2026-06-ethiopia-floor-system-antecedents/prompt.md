TASK

You are conducting an evidence-first historical research spike on the antecedents of structural floor-system acceptability in Ethiopia.

This is NOT a broad history memo.

CORE QUESTION

Before ribbed / hollow-concrete-block floor systems became ordinary in Ethiopian condominium and wider building practice, what earlier institutions, codes, design aids, imported technologies, demonstration projects, or teaching pathways made a nontraditional floor system thinkable and acceptable?

PRIMARY PURPOSE

Reconstruct the PRE-CODE / PRE-IHDP antecedents of acceptability for Ethiopian floor systems, with special attention to:

- ribbed slabs

- hollow concrete block slab systems

- precast beam-and-block systems

- one-way joist / ribbed floor systems

- related precast floor systems that may represent precursor or competing pathways

STRICT SCOPE

Main research window: 1970–1999

Sub-windows:

- 1960–1969 = lead-scan only, for imported actors or named buildings

- 1970–1982 = early code / engineering institutionalization

- 1983–1994 = ESCP era and pre-EBCS design culture

- 1984–1998 = PBPPE / prefabrication pathway

- 1999–2004 = boundary band to early low-cost housing transfer and early university normalization

Do NOT drift into a 2005–present adoption story except where later sources explicitly document earlier practice.

RESEARCH QUESTIONS

You must answer these separately:

A. PRE-PERMISSION

What evidence exists that Ethiopia already had a code, design-aid, or engineering basis for ribbed slabs or analogous floor systems before 1995?

B. PRECURSOR PATHWAYS

What earlier pathways existed for making a structural floor system acceptable in Ethiopia?

Examples:

- Ministry code or standard

- ESCP design aids

- AAU / Faculty of Technology teaching

- donor or bilateral technical assistance

- prefabrication authorities or state enterprises

- municipal demonstration projects

- imported engineering firms or foreign consultants

- supplier-led practice

C. SYSTEM DISTINCTION

Do not collapse these into one category:

- ribbed slab with filler block

- hollow block slab systems

- precast beam + block

- IMS or other precast frame systems

- steel-deck composite slabs

- prestressed hollow-core or other precast floors

For each source, state exactly which system family it refers to.

D. CODE VS PRACTICE

Separate:

- what was allowed in code or design aids

- what was taught

- what was piloted

- what was actually built

- what later sources only describe retrospectively

E. ORIGIN HYPOTHESES

Rank these hypotheses by evidence strength:

1. Ribbed/HCB systems were already permitted by inherited RC code/design logic before large government rollout.
