## Research Question

Before ribbed / hollow-concrete-block floor systems became ordinary in Ethiopian condominium and wider building practice, what earlier institutions, codes, design aids, imported technologies, demonstration projects, or teaching pathways made a nontraditional floor system thinkable and acceptable?

## High-Level Read

Both model generations converge on the most important answer:

The evidence does **not** justify forcing a 1960s origin story. The usable documentary trail begins mainly in the **1980s and early 1990s**, with the clearest pre-IHDP antecedents being:

1. Ethiopian structural-concrete code formation, especially ESCP-2:1983 and later EBCS-2:1995.
2. The 1991 Limit State Design Aids according to ESCP-2.
3. The Yugoslav / IMS / PBPPE prefabrication pathway, which made industrialized building systems institutionally thinkable but was not the same structural family as ribbed/HCB slabs.
4. The 1999–2003 GTZ low-cost housing transfer pathway, which introduced or documented the specific precast beam + hollow-block slab package.
5. AAU / Faculty of Technology thesis work by 1998–2004, which shows academic engagement with both IMS prefabrication and composite hollow-block / precast beam slab systems.

The two models agree on the core distinction:

- **Code/design permission** existed before IHDP.
- **Institutional prefabrication precedent** existed before IHDP.
- **Specific precast beam + HCB floor-system transfer** appears most clearly in the 1999–2004 GTZ / low-cost housing boundary band.
- The 1980s Yugoslav / PBPPE / IMS pathway is an antecedent of institutional acceptability, not a direct ancestor of the ribbed/HCB slab typology.

The ChatGPT generation is the safer base for synthesis because it repeatedly separates permission, teaching, piloting, actual construction, and retrospective description. It also keeps several claims at “partially supported” where original documents were not recovered.

The Gemini generation is useful because it produces a stronger causal architecture and emphasizes the triad of code formation, prefabrication transfer, and low-cost housing technology transfer. But it is more prone to overconfident wording, especially around robustness of the causal chain, certainty of direct support, and the rejection of fragmentation.

Best use:

- Use ChatGPT as the cautious evidence spine.
- Use Gemini as a causal-hypothesis generator.
- Source-clean the shared claims before producing synthesis.
- Downgrade Gemini’s stronger “directly supported” claims unless the underlying source is primary and page-level verified.

Reasoning: Both outputs identify the GTZ low-cost housing manual and Bole-Gerji / boundary-band work as the clearest bridge to the later condominium system. This is probably the strongest explanation for the specific Ethiopian beam + HCB package, but not for all ribbed slab permissibility.

### Hypothesis 3  
**Earlier prefabrication institutions created a permission template later reused for ribbed/HCB systems.**

Current rank: **Moderate as institutional antecedent; low as direct technical ancestor.**

Reasoning: PBPPE/IMS made state-led prefabrication, factory production, training, catalogs, standard details, and nontraditional assembly thinkable. But IMS is not a ribbed/HCB beam-and-block system.

### Hypothesis 4

Current rank: **Very high.**

Reasoning: This is the safest overall explanation. The evidence shows overlapping code, IMS, GTZ, school-standardization, university, composite steel, and hollow-core/adjoining pathways. Later normalization likely drew on multiple mechanisms.

### Hypothesis 5

Current rank: **Rejected only in the weak form; retained as caution in the strong form.**

Reasoning: The evidence is not too fragmented to identify meaningful antecedent mechanisms. But it is too fragmented to produce a clean linear origin story without major source cleanup.

## Provisional Synthesis

The safest synthesis is:

> The pre-IHDP acceptability of ribbed / HCB and related nontraditional floor systems in Ethiopia did not emerge from a single origin. The evidence does not support a forced 1960s story. The usable trail begins mainly in the 1980s, when Ethiopia developed a domestic structural-concrete code environment and imported a Yugoslav / IMS prefabrication system through PBPPE. That IMS pathway did not directly produce the later beam + HCB floor typology, but it did make industrialized, state-supported prefabrication institutionally thinkable. By 1991, Ethiopia had a limit-state design-aid layer tied to ESCP-2, and by 1995, EBCS-2 explicitly brought ribbed/hollow slab logic into Ethiopian concrete-code practice. The specific precast beam + hollow-block slab system that later fit the condominium pathway appears most clearly in the 1999–2003 GTZ low-cost housing transfer and the 2004 AAU composite slab thesis boundary band. The best explanation is therefore not one predecessor story, but a layered acceptability pathway: code permission, design-aid translation, prefabrication precedent, donor-assisted pilot transfer, and university normalization.

For Nebyu, the lesson is:

> A new nontraditional floor system probably needs more than a code argument. The Ethiopian record suggests that acceptability forms through a stack: recognized design logic, practical design aids, demonstration projects, institutional sponsorship, university validation, constructability guidance, and a supplier/training pathway. PBPPE shows that state-led industrialization alone is not enough if the system is too capital-intensive or mismatched to local construction constraints. GTZ’s beam + HCB pathway appears more relevant because it translated a structural idea into a low-formwork, low-capital, inspection-friendly, teachable, and repeatable construction package.
